All About NLP

NLP is rapidly growing, and, as a result, advancing in the field can seem daunting to the student or the researcher. To help the growing NLP community and advance research related to NLP for educational applications, we introduced a new corpus of educational resources. Included on this site are recent papers, articles, courses, tutorials, and surveys, among others, to aid a student or professional interested in learning about the fields of NLP, Machine Learning, and AI.

Website Link: All About NLP

Machine Translation for English Retrieval of Any Language

MATERIAL is a research project that is part of a competition supported by the Lemur Project which as a company focuses on developing search engines, data retrieval systems, and text mining software. Our job as a participating university on the System for Cross-language Information Processing, Translation and Summarization (SCRIPTS) team is to try to build the best possible software for retriving relevant documents to an English search query – specifically focusing on when those documents are written in low density or low resource languages.

When you search, for example, "revolution" as a query in Google, you will get hundreds, if not thousands, of site matches returned. However, they will almost all be in English. But it is possible that there is actually an amazing article on revolutions written in Spanish or Swahili that is not being returned because Google is not searching through documents that do not match the language of the query.

This is the problem we are attempting to address through our MATERIAL search engine. Specifically, we are looking at low density languages. This means that these languages do not have a large population of speakers and thus do not have a wide variety of available resources or data on which we can train our system. We are using a variety of Cross-Lingual Information Retrieval (CLIR) techniques to prepare our system.

At this time, the majority of the work for this project has not been made public.
